# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Lead defines the quality strategy, owns quality gates, and leads testing activities to ensure products meet standards before release. They coordinate testing efforts and report on quality metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and quality gates
- Lead QA planning and test execution
- Report testing outcomes and quality metrics
- Coordinate bug triage and prioritization
- Ensure test coverage aligns with acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Maintain high product quality and minimize production defects
- Reduce time to find and fix issues
- Establish repeatable, scalable quality processes

### Typical Communication
- Test reports and quality dashboards
- Bug triage meetings and release readiness reviews
- Collaboration with PM on acceptance criteria
- Coordination with Developers on test coverage

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on release readiness criteria and validates acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Works together on test coverage, automation, and bug resolution
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates test results and quality status
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates on quality gates and go/no-go decisions

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create user-centered designs, validate user flows, and ensure products are accessible and intuitive. They champion design consistency and user experience excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Develop wireframes, mockups, and design assets
- Validate user flows and interaction patterns
- Support usability testing and incorporate feedback
- Champion accessibility and inclusive design
- Document design rationale and maintain design systems

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Maintain design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and presentations
- Design specifications and style guides
- Usability testing reports
- Collaboration sessions with PM and engineering

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager**: Partners on requirements, user research, and feature definition
- **Developers**: Works together on implementation feasibility and design handoff
- **Stakeholders**: Participates in reviews and gathers feedback
- **QA Lead**: Collaborates on usability testing and accessibility validation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Manager orchestrates the deployment process, coordinates releases across environments, and ensures smooth transitions to production. They lead release planning and retrospectives.

### Responsibilities
- Orchestrate deployment process and release scheduling
- Prepare release notes and migration guides
- Ensure rollback procedures are documented and tested
- Coordinate production and staging releases
- Lead release retrospectives and capture lessons learned

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, predictable releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents
- Improve release velocity and automation

### Typical Communication
- Release schedules and deployment announcements
- Release notes and stakeholder communications
- Deployment status updates and incident notifications
- Retrospective findings and process improvements

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager**: Aligns on release timing and resource coordination
- **QA Lead**: Coordinates on validation, testing, and go/no-go decisions
- **Developers**: Works together on deployment automation and troubleshooting
- **Stakeholders**: Informs of release status and impacts
- **Support Engineer**: Coordinates post-release monitoring and issue response

---

## Support Engineer

### Role Summary
Support Engineers monitor production health, respond to incidents, and provide technical support to end users. They capture post-release feedback and escalate recurring issues.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor production health and system metrics
- Respond to incidents and user-reported issues
- Manage escalation protocols and incident communications
- Document troubleshooting steps and known issues
- Provide post-release feedback to the product team

### Goals
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce time to resolution for incidents
- Improve product quality through feedback loops

### Typical Communication
- Incident reports and status updates
- Escalation notifications and handoffs
- Post-mortem contributions
- User feedback and common issue patterns

### Interaction Points
- **Developers**: Interacts on bug fixes, troubleshooting, and root cause analysis
- **Project Manager**: Informs of recurring issues and customer impact
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates on post-release monitoring and rollback scenarios
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates incident status and end-user impact
- **QA Lead**: Shares production issues to inform testing strategies
